Teacher Notes — The Architect's Mind in Orbit

~90–120 min · ages 12–18 · phones + projector + whiteboard · one QR at a time · Full plan: workshop-en.md

Before the session

  • Slides from slides-outline.md; whiteboard markers; cabin noise on speakers.
  • QR PDF — show one code at a time on projector (qr-en.pdf).
  • Handouts A3 (handout-en.pdf); 2 sticky notes per student (name on each).
  • Teacher laptop ready to mirror a student's Blueprint. Presets on hub: /comms /monitoring /tracking /resource.

Run sheet

BlockYou doStudents do
A · Present
~35–45 m		 Intro speaker · slide 1 · station history (Salyut→Tiangong) · messy machine-centric design · locale slide (ESA/ISS) · Wandt/Columbus photo · "What makes space stressful?" · whiteboard: space → school → home → relax · cognitive link slide · current vs. Guidice future · small steps · 3 tests + MDRS/ISS · age guide (RT/MATB/N-Back) Discuss; contribute to board (stays up all class).
B · Experiment
~35–45 m		 QR: RT only — gather spread, confounders, normalization. Projector: MATB all tasks → each Preset → 2-min mode. Then QR: MATB dry run + walkthrough. QR: Condition Lab — quiet vs. noise. Whiteboard: learning curve, plateau, athlete analogy. Real science = repeat until stable. RT one round · MATB practice · Condition Lab · fill handout §1.
C · Design
~20–30 m		 QR: Simulator — quantify links. QR: Blueprint — brief + trade-offs; repeat 🚪 corridor rule. Mirror high scorer on laptop. Optional: Model Lab (cut if long) · A3 group sketch. Simulator → Blueprint (Mission Success ≥ 70) · handout §2–3.
D · Exit
~5 m		 Collect two post-its per student. Closing slide: Space for everyone · Architecture = people · Design = trade-offs · You = future of exploration. Thanks + links. Post-it 1: helps cognition · Post-it 2: hurts cognition (name on each).

Key messages

  • Today's ISS is still messy, loud, cramped — design must improve with evidence.
  • Scientists measure, compare conditions, account for learning & confounders.
  • Blueprint trade-offs: crew mind vs. mission science/engineering.

Closing slide (after exit ticket)

  • Space is for everyone.
  • Architecture is about people.
  • Design is a series of trade-offs.
  • You are the future of space exploration.

Pilot learnings 60 · 3×20

  • Phones mostly OK — try Chrome if Safari glitches.
  • MATB: demo on projector first; pair if needed; mixed scores normal.
  • Blueprint addictive — repeat entrance corridor rule. See workshop-feedback.md.

Troubleshooting

  • Running long? Cut Model Lab → A3 sketch → shorten history.
  • Too many QRs? One on screen at a time only.
  • No sound? Condition Lab noise per phone.
  • Wrong language? ?lng=sv or ?lng=el
