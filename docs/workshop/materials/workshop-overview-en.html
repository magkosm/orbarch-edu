The Architect's Mind in Orbit

Classroom workshop · Orbital Architecture · KTH & ESERO · v2.1.1
For educators running the session: use teacher-notes-en.pdf on the day. This sheet is for coordinators, schools, and train-the-trainer briefings.

Summary

A ~90–120 minute hands-on workshop for ages 12–18 (pilot: Grade 8/9). Students use their own phones; the teacher needs a projector and whiteboard. The theme is how space-station architecture affects crew cognition — measured with the same research tools used on the ISS and at Mars analog missions.

Live app: magkosm.github.io/orbarch-edu · Languages: EN / SV / EL (?lng=sv / ?lng=el)

Learning objectives

  • Name environmental factors that raise or lower stress — in space and in everyday life.
  • Explain how scientists measure cognition and compare conditions (baseline vs. stressor).
  • Apply design trade-offs: crew cognition vs. mission science and engineering.

Session flow

BlockTimeWhat happens
A · Presentation~35–45 minStation history, ISS reality (Wandt/Columbus photo), whiteboard stress discussion, cognitive link, how we test cognition, MDRS & ISS research context.
B · Experiments~35–45 minReaction Time warm-up → MATB taught on projector → student dry run → Condition Lab (quiet vs. noise) → learning-effect discussion.
C · Design studio~20–30 minArchitecture Simulator → Habitat Blueprint Designer (trade-offs, corridor-to-entrance rule) → optional Model Lab or A3 sketch.
D · Close~5 minTwo sticky notes per student (helps / hurts cognition) · closing slide · thanks.

What you need

  • Projector + slide deck (from slides-outline.md)
  • Wi-Fi; student phones (Chrome preferred over Safari)
  • Printed QR sheet — one QR on screen at a time
  • A3 handouts (1 per student), 2 sticky notes each, whiteboard markers
  • Room speakers for cabin noise (or in-app noise in Condition Lab)
  • Teacher laptop to mirror a high-scoring Blueprint layout

  • Architecture is about people.
  • Design is a series of trade-offs.
  • You are the future of space exploration.
